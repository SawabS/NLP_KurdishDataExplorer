# The Kurdish Language Corpus: State of the Art (Azzat et al. 2023)

## Summary

Media Azzat, Karwan Jacksi, and Ismael Ali (University of Zakho) survey the
available textual corpora across Kurdish dialects and scripts. They conclude that,
despite growing online content, Kurdish suffers from a lack of unified orthography
and well-organized annotated resources, and they explicitly enumerate open
problems and future directions. Published in the *Science Journal of University of
Zakho*, 11(1), 127–133 (2023).

## Key claims

- Machine-readable Kurdish text is the first step toward text mining and semantic
  web applications (translation, information retrieval, recommendation), yet such
  text is scarce.
- The core obstacles are the absence of a **unified orthography** and a shortage
  of annotated corpora across the dialect/script landscape.
- The paper is not just a catalogue: it lists open problems (dialectal
  fragmentation, missing annotation standards) and suggests future directions.

## Evidence

- Surveys both Arabic-based and Latin-based corpora across multiple Kurdish
  dialects.
- Notes the morphological complexity of Sorani and the lack of capitalization in
  the Arabic-based orthography as complicating factors.
- DOI 10.25271/sjuoz.2023.11.1.1123.
